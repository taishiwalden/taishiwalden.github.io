How to Set Up Google Search Console for Your Website

Learn how to use Google’s free web services tools to measure your site's web traffic, page performance, fix mobile site issues, and rank higher in search engines.

Google Search Console (GSC)

is an extremely useful tool for business owners and marketing professionals to track site performance, core web vitals, mobile usability, and getting Google to index your pages. While it’s not required to verify your ownership of your website domain, verified users are granted access to an extended suite of data and tools. If this sounds overwhelming, don’t worry, we’ll teach you all of the basics in this article—including how to set up, verify ownership, and interpret simple metrics and key performance indicators on Google Search Console.

What is Google Search Console? Google Search Console is a free web service offered by Google to help monitor, maintain, and troubleshoot a website’s online presence on Google’s search engine. It has a variety of tools and reports to help you understand and improve how Google sees your site. Most notably, GSC offers reports that help you do the following: Troubleshoot issues for Accelerated Mobile Pages (AMP), mobile usability, and other search features, which is paramount if your website gets a lot of mobile traffic (ie. you own a restaurant and/or bar). Confirm that Google search engine can find and crawl your site. Fix indexing problems and request re-indexing of new or updated content. View Google Search traffic data for your site: how often your site appears in Google search, which search queries show your site, how often searchers click through for those queries, and more. Show you which sites link to your website.

How can it benefit my business?

Gain insight into core web vitals: with the Performance Report, you are able to view important metrics like the average position in search results; click-through rate (CTR); and any special features (such as rich results or SERP snippets) associated with your website search results. You can use this information to improve your site’s SEO ranking and search performance. The key uses of the Performance Report are: Detects which of your web pages have the highest (and lowest) CTR from Google search results. See how your search traffic changes over time, where it’s coming from (ex. paid or organic search), and what search queries are most likely to show your website. Learn which search queries are made on smartphones to improve your mobile marketing.

Optimize for mobile users

To have high authority online, you must optimize your website’s content for mobile users, especially if you own a brick-and-mortar business. The majority of people searching for “restaurant near me” or the like are using mobile devices, and yet a lot of restaurant websites do not have responsive designs. This is not only a headache for users, but can result in lost business opportunities. Measure the indexing of your web pages.

The Index Coverage report shows whether your website pages (or specific URLs) have been crawled and indexed by Google. The summary page shows results for all URLs in your property grouped by status (e.g. valid, error, warning).

What to pay attention to

The number of valid indexed pages should increase gradually as your site grows. If you see drops or spikes in the Index Coverage report while monitoring your website, you can refer to Google Search Console’s troubleshooting section.

How do I set up my website with Google Search Console?

To add your website to GSC, navigate to Google Search Console and sign in, and click “Add Property”. You will then be prompted to choose a property type: Domain property and URL prefix property. What’s the difference? A domain property requires DNS record verification, meaning it includes subdomains such as m, www, and multiple protocols: HTTP, HTTPS, FTP. On the other hand, a URL prefix property supports several verification types. For this reason, DNS verification is one of the best methods to verify your site because you won’t have to verify your property again, in case you need to switch from HTTP to HTTPS or www to non-www in the future. Verify your domain

On the left-hand tab, click “settings” > “Ownership verification” > and input your domain DNS from your server provider. Once your property has been verified, you’re ready to start measuring your website’s performance! You can check indexed pages, submit broken links, submit new content to be indexed, identify crawling errors, and more.

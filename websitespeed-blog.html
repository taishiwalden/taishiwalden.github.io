How to Check Your Website Speed ⚡️

  1. Home
  2. Blog
  3. How to Check Your Website Speed ⚡️

Three industry methods to measure your web performance, and how to improve it.

Website speed, or performance, refers to how quickly a browser is able to load fully functional web pages from a given site. It’s the first impression your website has on users, and speedy websites can directly increase sales and decrease bounce rates (the percentage of visitors who enter the site and then leave, rather than continuing to view other pages within the same site).

Websites with poor performance not only drive users away, but they can also result in poor search engine rankings, negative user experiences, and potentially fewer future customers. Is your website fast or slow? This article will explain why speed matters and three free tools to measure your website performance.

Even if you have the most beautiful responsive layout and design, it doesn’t matter if people leave your website after waiting for it to load. Many users on mobile devices are likely to close the window or click away if a page does not load within a few seconds. There are a variety of web presence analysis tools out there where you can calculate performance indicators like user experience, conversion rates, SEO rankings, and more. I’ve compiled a shortlist of industry-standard tools that web professionals use to measure their sites.

PageSpeed Insights (PSI) is a free developer tool offered by Google to analyze a web page's content and then generate suggestions to make that page faster. It reports on both mobile and desktop devices and provides data that is useful for debugging performance issues and measuring Core Web Vitals.

Developed by Carbon60, GTmetrix is a tool for customers to easily test the performance of their web pages. GTmetrix has become one of the most popular methods to assess website performance. You can test your site in different countries, browsers, and connection speeds to see how your pages load for different users. They also offer performance monitoring and notifications for when your site is slow.

Pingdom is an easy-to-use tool that allows web developers, both experts and novices alike, to analyze their website loading time. Using their free Website Speed Test you can test your website from 7 different countries, and identify bottlenecks on your websites' uptime and performance.

Ignite Local is a digital agency that offers online marketing services geared towards small businesses. They offer a free Web Presence Scorecard on their website, which returns an SEO report card, Rank Analysis, Website Accessibility, and more. Additionally, their rank analysis gives insights on how many links, backlinks, and competitor links your website has, which the other tools do not offer. This is particularly useful if your business is trying to increase your online presence through search engine optimization.

Now that you’ve learned the basics of website speed and the tools to analyze it, now it’s time to take action to optimize your website. Depending on what platform/server your website is built with, the methods to increase your page speeds may differ.
